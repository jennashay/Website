Blood in the Water: Attica Prison Uprising of 1971 And Why it Matters Today
Challenge
To design a poster for the School of Communication at RIT that informs the university population of an upcoming guest lecture and encourages people to come with its engaging design.
Solution
A deep red stripe motif running horizontally between the viewer and the author’s image doubles as both a reference to prison bars as well as the blood shed during th prison uprising. Gotham was chosen for its neutral nature, which references academia and retrospection, while also commanding authority in bar-like all caps. Black, white, and red tied the text, bar motif, author portrait, and book image together in a cohesive and dramatic color scheme.
